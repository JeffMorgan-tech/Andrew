SCENE 1 Cold Open — Andrew's Home Office, Monday Morning
Fade in on a home office. It's organized, lived-in, and distinctly "guy who's been doing this for a while." Dual monitors. A headset hanging from a monitor arm. A mug that reads "HAVE YOU TRIED TURNING IT OFF AND ON AGAIN?" — the mug that will become iconic by Season 3. ANDREW sits at his desk, coffee in hand, scrolling through tickets. The morning sun hits the blinds. Everything is calm. Everything is peaceful. This will be the last moment of peace for the entire season.
♪ TEAMS CALL RINGTONE ♪
It's RACHELLE. ANDREW clicks accept. RACHELLE appears on screen — soft lighting, organized bookshelf, the gentle authority of a woman who has never needed to raise her voice because the first time was always enough.
Rachelle
Good morning, Andrew. Do you have a minute?
Andrew
Yeah, of course. What's up?
Rachelle
So, I have an opportunity for you.
ANDREW freezes. In corporate, "opportunity" never means opportunity. It means more work dressed up in a nicer outfit.
Andrew
(cautiously) ...An opportunity.
Rachelle
We've got two new hires starting this week. Luke and Anthony. Both remote. Both brand new to support. And I'd love for you to take them under your wing. Show them the ropes. Be their mentor.
Andrew
Both of them? At the same time?
Rachelle
I know it's a lot. Normally I'd split them between you and Jeff, but Jeff is... well, you know Jeff.
Andrew
(knowing exactly) He's on the project.
Rachelle
He's on the project. He built some kind of — I think it's a knowledge system? He sent me a thirty-page document about it last week. With diagrams. And a timeline. I stopped reading on page four but I told him it was very thorough.
Laugh track
Andrew
So Jeff's out, and I've got two rookies.
Rachelle
(warm, encouraging) You're the best person for this, Andrew. You've been on the desk a couple of years now, you know the systems inside and out, and honestly — the team respects you. The new guys are going to need someone steady. Someone patient.
Andrew
(flattery working, against his better judgment) ...Alright. I'll do it.
Rachelle
(beaming) Wonderful. They start tomorrow. I'll send you their onboarding packets.
Andrew
Wait — tomorrow?
Rachelle
(already ending the call) You're going to be great! Talk soon!
♪ TEAMS CALL ENDED ♪
Andrew
(staring at the blank screen, coffee getting cold) ...Tomorrow.
Big laugh
♪ THEME SONG — TITLE SEQUENCE ♪
SCENE 2 Act One — "Meet the New Guys"
Tuesday morning. 9:00 AM. ANDREW has set up a welcome call. He's prepared a clean agenda, a shared OneNote, and a positive attitude. The call is scheduled for nine sharp. At 9:00, only ANDREW is on the call. At 9:05, still just ANDREW. At 9:09, a webcam flickers on.
LUKE appears. He is in bed. Not near a bed. Not on a bed. In bed. Covers pulled up. Hair in four directions. Behind him, a ceiling fan spins lazily. He squints at the screen like a man who has just discovered what sunlight is.
Luke
(voice cracking) Hey... hey, is this the — is this the meeting?
Andrew
(processing what he's seeing) ...Hey, Luke. Yeah, this is it. Welcome to the team.
Luke
Awesome. Awesome. Sorry I'm a little late, I was — I had a whole thing with my alarm. It's a time zone issue.
Andrew
We're in the same time zone, Luke.
Luke
(without missing a beat) Right, but my alarm isn't.
Big laugh
At 9:14, ANTHONY's camera turns on. He's in a car. Seatbelt on. Something that looks suspiciously like a Starbucks drive-thru is visible through the rear window. He's wearing sunglasses indoors — well, in a car — and smiling brightly.
Anthony
Hey! Good morning! So sorry — I'm totally at my desk. Just had a quick errand. But I'm here! Fully present!
Andrew
Anthony... are you driving?
Anthony
(looking around as if surprised by his own surroundings) What? No! This is my... home office. It's got a very open floor plan.
Laugh track
Andrew
I can see a Starbucks sign behind you.
Anthony
(glancing over his shoulder, then back) That's a... motivational poster. My wife put it up. She loves coffee culture. As decor.
Big laugh
Andrew
(closing his eyes, breathing deeply, then opening them with a smile that costs him physically) Okay. Great. Well — welcome, both of you. I'm Andrew. I'll be your mentor. I'm going to help you get acclimated to the team, the tools, and what remote work actually looks like here. Any questions before we start?
Luke
Yeah — is there a dress code?
Andrew
You're in bed, Luke.
Luke
Right, so... no dress code. Got it.
Big laugh
SCENE 3 "Remote Work 101"
ANDREW has begun the orientation. He's sharing his screen, walking through the basics: the ticketing system, the Slack channels, the Teams structure, the knowledge base. LUKE and ANTHONY are on camera. LUKE has at least moved to a desk — though the desk appears to be a TV tray. ANTHONY has arrived home and is at an actual setup, Starbucks cup prominently in frame.
Andrew
Alright, so — remote work. It's awesome. Genuinely. No commute, flexible setup, work in your own space. But there's a catch. And the catch is: nobody can see you working. Which means you've gotta be intentional about it. Show up to calls on time. Keep your status updated. Respond to messages within a reasonable window. The work is the same as in-office — the only difference is you have to prove you're doing it.
Luke
(nodding seriously) Totally. Yeah. Makes sense.
Anthony
For sure. Very clear.
A pause. Neither of them has taken a single note. ANDREW looks at their screens. LUKE appears to have a YouTube tab open. ANTHONY is stirring his coffee.
Andrew
Are either of you writing this down?
Luke
Oh, I have a photographic memory.
Andrew
What was the last thing I said?
Luke
(long pause) ...Something about being... intentional?
Laugh track
Anthony
I figured we'd get a recording of this.
Andrew
We're not recording this. This is a conversation.
Anthony
(pulling out his phone) Want me to record it on my phone?
Andrew
(staring into the void) No. I want you to take notes. With a pen. Or a keyboard. Like people have done since the beginning of time.
Luke
(opening Notepad on his computer) Okay. Ready. Go ahead.
Andrew
I've been talking for twenty minutes, Luke.
Luke
Right. So I'll just put "see first twenty minutes" and start from here.
Big laugh
SCENE 4 Act Two — "Meanwhile, at Jeff's Desk"
JEFF M.'s home office. Three monitors. Treadmill desk. A whiteboard behind him covered in diagrams, sticky notes, and what appears to be a hand-drawn system architecture with at least forty nodes. He is walking at a brisk pace and typing simultaneously. His phone buzzes. It's ANDREW on a DM.
Andrew
(via Teams DM) Jeff. I need help. The new guys are... a lot. Can you take one of them? Even just for the week?
Jeff M.
(voice message, slightly out of breath from the treadmill) Andrew. Brother. I would love to. Truly. But I am at a critical juncture with the project. I'm building a metadata pipeline that syncs forty thousand knowledge articles through a SharePoint REST API using FedAuth cookie authentication. I wrote a schema last night with three tables and a full-text index. I haven't slept in a way that would be considered medically appropriate. Also, I sent Rachelle a progress update this morning. It was eleven pages. She responded with a thumbs-up emoji. I'm choosing to interpret that as enthusiastic support.
Big laugh
Andrew
(typing) So that's a no.
Jeff M.
(voice message) It's a "not right now but I believe in you." Also, I looked up both new hires on LinkedIn. Luke's profile picture is a selfie from what appears to be a bowling alley. Anthony's headline says "Future CEO." I have concerns about both of them but I've documented my concerns in a spreadsheet that I'll send to Brenda.
Andrew
(typing) Please do not send anything to Brenda. You don't even know who Brenda is.
Jeff M.
(voice message) I found her on the org chart. She's in HR. She seems important. I've already emailed her twice. She hasn't responded yet but I think we're building a rapport.
Big laugh
SCENE 5 "The First Day Continues"
Back on the orientation call. ANDREW is now walking LUKE and ANTHONY through their first ticket. He's screen sharing the ticketing system. A real, simple, P4 ticket. "Customer needs password reset." The most basic task in support.
Andrew
Okay, this is a great starter ticket. Customer needs a password reset. Luke, walk me through what you'd do.
Luke
(leaning in, studying the screen) Okay so... I would... email them?
Andrew
Email them what?
Luke
Their password?
Andrew
(carefully) We don't... we don't have their password, Luke. We reset it. Through the admin portal. And then we send them a temporary one.
Luke
Oh. Right. Yeah. I knew that. The admin portal.
Andrew
Do you know where the admin portal is?
Luke
(staring at screen, clearly lost) ...Is it the blue one?
Laugh track
Andrew
Anthony, what about you?
Anthony
(confidently) I'd Google it.
The words land like a prophecy. ANDREW doesn't know it yet, but this sentence will haunt him for four more episodes.
Andrew
(blinking) ...We'll work on it.
Big laugh
SCENE 6 Act Three — "The Pep Talk"
End of Day 1. ANDREW is on a private call with TINA. She's in her usual setup — bright, organized, a succulent garden visible in the background. She's already heard about the new hires through the team grapevine.
Tina
So! How'd it go?! Tell me everything!
Andrew
Tina, one of them was in bed. The other was in a Starbucks drive-thru. Neither of them took notes. One of them asked if we could just email customers their passwords. The other said he'd "Google it." It was their first day and I already need a vacation.
Tina
(undeterred, still smiling) Okay, but that's normal! When I started in the field, I didn't know how to read a calibration log for the first three weeks! My supervisor — Linda, you'd love Linda — she just kept saying, "Give it time, Tina. They'll get there." And she was right!
Andrew
Did any of your field trainees join their first day from bed?
Tina
(thinking) Well, no. But we had a guy show up in flip-flops to a hospital install once, so... same energy?
Laugh track
Andrew
I don't know, Tina. Maybe I'm not cut out for this. I'm a support guy. I fix tickets. I'm not a teacher.
Tina
(leaning into camera, genuine) Andrew. You are a teacher. Every time you walk someone through a ticket, every time you explain a process — that's teaching. You've been doing it the whole time. You just had easier students before.
Audience: "Aww"
Andrew
(small smile) ...Thanks, Tina.
Tina
Also, if it helps, Devon said he'd "rather eat glass than mentor anyone," so the bar is literally on the floor. You're already winning.
Big laugh
SCENE 7 Tag — End of Day
5:30 PM. ANDREW at his desk. He closes his laptop halfway, then stops. Opens his email. There's a message from LUKE. Subject: "Day 1 Notes." ANDREW opens it, expecting nothing. The email reads: "Hey Andrew — thanks for today. I know I wasn't great but I'm going to be better tomorrow. I wrote down everything I could remember. It's not much but it's a start. — Luke." Attached is a Word document. It's one page. The formatting is terrible. But it's there.
Andrew
(reading, surprised) ...Huh.
A second email appears. From ANTHONY. Subject: "Quick question." The body reads: "Hey, where's the admin portal again? I want to practice the password reset thing before tomorrow. Also — sorry about the car thing. I'll be at my desk next time. For real." ANDREW exhales. Types back to both of them: "See you at nine. Sharp."
He leans back in his chair. Looks around his home office. Picks up his mug. Takes a sip. It's cold. He drinks it anyway.
Andrew
(to himself, half-smiling) Remote work is fun.
He opens his desk drawer. Inside: a travel brochure for Fiji. He looks at it. Shuts the drawer.
Andrew
Not yet.
Applause — warm, the first of many
