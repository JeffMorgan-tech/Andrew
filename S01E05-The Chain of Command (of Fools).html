SCENE 4 Act Three — "The Proper Way (In Theory)"
ANDREW has regained control of the screen share. He has added a new slide to his presentation. It reads: "ESCALATION IS NOT YOUR FIRST OPTION. IT IS YOUR LAST RESORT." Below it, in smaller text: "YES, LUKE, YOU STILL HAVE TO TRY FIRST."
Andrew
Okay. Let's reset. Here is the correct escalation workflow. Step one: Customer reports an issue. Step two: You — the Tier 1 agent — investigate. Check the knowledge base. Check known issues. Reproduce the problem if you can. Step three: If you still can't resolve it, you document everything you've tried. And I mean everything. Not "I looked at it." Not "Seemed broken." Actual steps. Actual results. Step four: Only then do you submit the escalation request through the proper channel, with your documentation attached.
Tina
(taking enthusiastic notes) This is exactly like field work! We had a checklist — engine status, error codes, calibration logs — all before we could radio back to the home office. If you called in without your checklist, they'd just say "call us back when you've got it" and hang up. (laughing fondly) One time I forgot the calibration log and my supervisor made me drive forty-five minutes back to the site to get it. In a snowstorm! Best lesson I ever learned.
