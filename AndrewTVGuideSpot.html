● REC TV GUIDE — 1993 FALL PREVIEW CH 04 — TGIF LINEUP
★ TV Guide Featured Listing ★
Andrew
Software Support Specialist
Fridays 8:00 PM ET — Channel 04 TGIF Lineup
TV-PG | 22 min | Comedy / Workplace / Sitcom
Created by Jeff & Claude | Friday Night Productions
When mild-mannered software support specialist Andrew agrees to mentor two remote new hires, he discovers that teaching people to close tickets is easy — teaching them to show up on time, stay off camera at Starbucks, and stop eating burritos during customer calls is the real challenge. Joined by a colorful cast of coworkers spread across the country, the team navigates remote work life with heart, humor, and an alarming number of emails to Brenda in HR.
Season 1 Episode Guide — 13 Episodes
E01 "Remote Work is Fun?"
Rachelle offers Andrew the "opportunity" to mentor two new hires at once — because Jeff is too deep in his pet project to help. Luke shows up in bed. Anthony shows up in a Starbucks drive-thru. Neither takes notes. The Fiji brochure appears for the first time.
series premiere
E02 "New Hires.."
Three weeks in and Andrew isn't seeing progress. He seeks advice from the mentor squad — Tina says celebrate small wins, Devon says accept the void, Jeff sends a 27-page curriculum to Brenda. Andrew tries all three approaches at once. Anthony sends a customer the Google homepage.
E03 "Corine Don't Play"
Corine's P1 customer Marion DeFabuloso is furious. Andrew steps in to deescalate and brings the newbs to shadow an engineering meeting with Mike Shimmy. Luke eats a burrito on camera. He drops the burrito. He unmutes to announce he dropped the burrito. Corine's pen goes perpendicular.
guest: Mike Shimmy & Marion DeFabuloso
E04 "Away From Keyboard"
Andrew finds both new hires completely checked out on a Tuesday morning. Luke invents "pre-resolution" — visualizing the fix without actually doing it. Anthony's Alexa apparently orders oat milk and honks. Andrew creates a presentation called "WHAT YOUR JOB IS: A Refresher." Slide 1: "Be awake."
E05 "The Chain of Command (of Fools)"
The team learns proper internal escalation. Jeff builds a 47-node flowchart that accounts for Mercury retrograde and sends it to the VP of Operations. Devon has memorized KB-4471 from fixing it four hundred times. Anthony quietly follows the process correctly and everyone is stunned.
full ensemble debut: Tina, Devon, Jeff M.
E06 "Reply All My Enemies"
Greg from SDS publicly mocks the remote team in Slack and singles out Tina by name. Andrew brings it to Rachelle. Corine delivers a 7-page evidence file with footnotes that reference an appendix. Jeff deploys the Spongebob text. Rachelle is furious — professionally. HR investigation opens.
debut: Rachelle & Corine
E07 "The Mute Button Exists for a Reason"
Luke's kettle chips derail the Thursday standup at 85 decibels. Carlos gives the most effective "cool uncle" talk in mentoring history. Corine creates a food tier list for meetings. Jeff conducts a four-brand chip acoustics study and produces the 14-page Unified Snack Protocol. Devon heard the guacamole.
debut: Carlos & Juwan
E08 "Out of Office"
The Memorial Day episode. Tina unpacks boxes in Cleveland (she misses Publix). Andrew escapes to a cabin in Wisconsin. Devon grills his mom's chicken recipe in Vermont. Rachelle tends baby chicks and turkeys in Texas — Dolly graduated to the outdoor run, Big Tex fought the hose and tied. Anthony games for 52 straight hours. Luke tests auto-mute headsets. Jeff's three-year-old tried to eat a sparkler.
holiday episode
E09 "Andrew Knows Best"
Andrew overrides Anthony's correct approach on a ticket and breaks the customer's system — exactly what Juwan warned about. He calls Juwan, owns the mistake, and the newbs watch the recovery. Rachelle tells him the best thing a mentor can do is let students see them be wrong. She compares him to Dolly the chicken. He doesn't love that.
E10 "Panic! At the Helpdesk"
A faulty patch takes down dozens of customer sites. 38 P1 tickets and climbing. The team is on nonstop calls with no breaks — Andrew juggles triage and mentoring until he's running on fumes. Tina reads the patch notes line-by-line, finds the real culprit (a session timeout change), and saves the day. She takes Luke and Anthony under her wing to teach field thinking.
E11 "Email Distribution Lists"
Someone anonymously reply-alls to the CEO's company-wide email — not with text, but with a meme: a cow holding a sign that reads "This isn't a TV show, Greg." Jeff goes full detective — creates a Slack investigation channel, invents an IT form, tracks login timestamps and session tokens. Corine approves the investigation, not the process. The culprit? Brenda from HR, who was trying to send the Greg meme to her friend Pam. She's been a secret Greg hater this whole time. The cow lives forever.
E12 "Fly Little Birds" — Part 1
The newbs take calls on their own. Andrew watches from the sidelines, tying his self-worth to their performance. Then his internet drops during a P1 bridge call with SDS. Anthony is alone on the line — site down, customer waiting, and Greg is there. To be continued...
season finale — part 1
E13 "Fly Little Birds" — Part 2
Devon turns his camera on for the first time all season to put Greg in his place. Carlos talks Luke through the chaos. The team rallies. Andrew's internet comes back — but the newbs already handled it. Post-credits: Jeff's project makes a breakthrough. Luke muted his mic to sneeze. They flew.
season finale — part 2
