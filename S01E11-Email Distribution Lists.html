SCENE 1 Cold Open — Thursday, 8:47 AM
ANDREW logs in. Normal morning. Coffee. Tickets. The gentle hum of remote work. Then his Outlook pings. And pings again. And pings six more times in rapid succession. He opens his inbox. There are forty-three new emails. All from the same thread. The original email was a company-wide announcement from the CEO — a standard quarterly update sent to the global distribution list. Reply-all is supposed to be disabled on this list. It is, in fact, famously disabled. IT locked it down two years ago after the Great Reply-All Incident of 2022, which reportedly generated four hundred responses in ninety minutes and crashed the Exchange server.
And yet. Someone has replied all. To the entire company. And the reply — sent from what appears to be an anonymous or masked address — contains no text. Just an image. A meme. A cow — a Holstein dairy cow, standing upright, holding a sign that reads: "This isn't a TV show, Greg." No context. No explanation. Just a cow, a message, and three crying-laughing emojis. And every single person on the support team knows exactly which Greg this is about.
From: SHS_Internal_User
To: [email protected]
Subject: RE: Q2 Company Update — A Message from Leadership
[IMAGE: A Holstein cow holding a sign that reads "This isn't a TV show, Greg."]

😂😂😂
ANDREW stares at it. The entire company — thousands of people — has just received a meme roasting Greg from SDS. From someone no one can identify. On a list that shouldn't allow replies. Andrew's mouth falls open. Then — slowly — the corner of his mouth turns up.
Andrew
(quietly, fighting a grin) ...Oh no.
